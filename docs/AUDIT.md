# AI Employees Landing Page Audit

## PART 1 — SITE AUDIT

### 1) Positioning Problems

- **Generic product name**: "AI Employees" is broad and competes with many AI tools. Doesn't clearly signal "receptionist for service businesses" which is the primary use case.
- **No clear category**: Visitors searching "AI receptionist" or "AI answering service" may not immediately recognize this is the right product.
- **Value prop buried**: The strongest differentiator—industry-specific AI (dentist, real estate, med spa)—appears late. Hero focuses on "digital employees" rather than outcomes.
- **Feature-led, not outcome-led**: Copy describes what the product does ("answers customers") instead of business results ("stop losing bookings to missed calls").

### 2) SEO Problems

- **Thin content**: No long-form content sections. AI search and Google favor pages with substantial, authoritative content.
- **Missing target pages**: No dedicated pages for "ai receptionist," "ai receptionist for dentists," "best ai receptionist," etc.—high-intent queries.
- **Single H1**: Only one H1. GEO and SEO benefit from clear hierarchical structure with semantic headings.
- **No internal linking**: No blog, no pillar pages, no hub structure for topical authority.
- **Schema incomplete**: Only SoftwareApplication. Missing FAQPage, Organization, Product, HowTo—schema types LLMs reference.
- **Keywords not in body**: Target keywords appear in meta but not in prominent, crawlable content blocks.

### 3) Conversion Problems

- **Single CTA type**: Only "Join Beta" and "See Demo." No "Book Demo," "Start Automation," or "See How It Works" for different funnel stages.
- **Form too minimal**: Missing phone, business type, monthly inquiries—fields that qualify leads and increase commitment.
- **No urgency**: "Limited spots" is weak. No scarcity, social proof, or risk reversal.
- **Beta-first friction**: Cold traffic may resist "beta." "Book Demo" or "See Pricing" often converts better.
- **No video/demo preview**: "See Demo" goes to anchor. No embedded video or interactive walkthrough.
- **Pricing opacity**: $99 beta vs $399 regular—no clear value framing or comparison to alternatives.

### 4) Missing Trust Signals

- **No testimonials**: Placeholder text only. No real customer quotes.
- **No case studies**: No "Dentist X increased bookings by 40%" type stories.
- **No logos**: "Logo" placeholders. No named customers or partners.
- **No metrics**: No "500+ businesses" or "2M conversations handled."
- **No security detail**: "Secure infrastructure" is vague. No SOC 2, encryption, or compliance mentions.
- **No integrations list**: Supported channels listed but no CRM, calendar, or tool integrations shown.

### 5) Structural Problems

- **Section order suboptimal**: Trust bar (channels) comes before problem. Social proof comes after CTA. Standard flow: Hero → Trust → Problem → Solution → Social proof → CTA.
- **No comparison section**: Visitors comparing options have no "vs competitors" or "vs manual process" content.
- **FAQ collapsed**: Answers hidden by default. GEO and SEO benefit from visible, scannable Q&A.
- **No sticky CTA**: Mobile users must scroll to convert. No floating or sticky CTA.
- **Footer minimal**: No sitemap, legal, or resource links for SEO.

### 6) Missing AI-Search (GEO) Optimization

- **No explicit product definition**: LLMs need clear statements like "AI Employee is an AI-powered automation platform that acts like a digital staff member."
- **No "What is X?" sections**: Missing "What is an AI Receptionist?"—high-citation content.
- **No comparison content**: "AI Receptionist vs Human Receptionist," "AI Receptionist vs Missed Calls"—content LLMs often cite.
- **No structured benefits**: Benefits scattered. LLMs prefer clear bullet lists with explicit claims.
- **No use-case examples**: Industry examples exist but lack "Examples of Use" framing with clear problem-solution-outcome.
- **FAQ answers too short**: GEO favors comprehensive, quotable answers. Current answers are brief.
- **No dedicated GEO pages**: No /ai-receptionist, /ai-receptionist-for-dentists, etc.
